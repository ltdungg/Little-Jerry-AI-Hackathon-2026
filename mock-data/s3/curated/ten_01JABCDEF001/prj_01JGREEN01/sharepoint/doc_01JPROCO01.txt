Green Hope Foundation — Procurement Policy 2026

Section 1: General Principles

1.1 All procurement activities must comply with donor requirements and local regulations.
1.2 Transparency and accountability are fundamental to all purchasing decisions.
1.3 Competitive bidding is required for all purchases exceeding $1,000.
1.4 All procurement records must be maintained for a minimum of 5 years.

Section 2: Roles and Responsibilities

2.1 Project Managers are responsible for initiating procurement requests within their project budget.
2.2 The Finance Officer reviews all purchase requests for budget compliance.
2.3 The Executive Director provides final approval for purchases exceeding $5,000.
2.4 The Board of Directors must approve all purchases exceeding $25,000.

Section 3: Procurement Thresholds

3.1 Petty Cash (under $100): Single receipt, Project Manager approval
3.2 Minor Purchase ($100–$500): Single quote, Project Manager approval
3.3 Standard Purchase ($500–$1,000): Two quotes, Project Manager + Finance approval
3.4 Competitive Bid ($1,000–$5,000): Three quotes, dual signature required
3.5 Major Purchase ($5,000–$25,000): Formal tender process, Executive Director approval
3.6 Strategic Purchase (over $25,000): Board approval required

Section 4: Approval Process

4.1 Purchases under $500: Project Manager approval
4.2 Purchases $500–$5,000: Dual signature required (Project Manager + Finance)
4.3 Purchases over $5,000: Board approval required

Section 5: Documentation Requirements

5.1 All purchases require a purchase order (PO) number.
5.2 Receipts must be attached to the expense report within 30 days.
5.3 Vendor invoices must be matched against purchase orders before payment.
5.4 Any procurement exception must be documented and approved by the Executive Director.

Section 6: Vendor Management

6.1 Preferred vendors should be reviewed annually.
6.2 New vendors must be vetted before first purchase.
6.3 Conflict of interest declarations are required for all procurement decisions.
6.4 Gift and hospitality policies apply to all vendor interactions.
